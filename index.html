02 / 07    Observations

3 things I noticed.

01

Your counter-narrative posts outperform your educational posts by 10x.

When you challenge a belief your audience already holds - "being overly nice hides your true feelings," "feeling stuck is impatience driven by fear" - your account goes viral. When you explain a framework, you lose people. Same account. Same effort. Very different entry point.

321,544 views (overly nice reel) vs. 58 likes (ACT therapy explainer). The gap isn't about quality of thinking - it's about which thought comes first.

The opportunity: shift the content ratio toward reframe-first. Lead with the contradiction, not the concept. The insight you already have just needs a different door.

02

Forward Shift launched in February. Its content funnel is 3 posts thin.

Your newest program is open for applications right now. Across 100 pieces of content, I found 2-3 dedicated posts for it. No origin story for why you built it. No "who is this for" post. No FAQ. The program itself is strong - the content supporting it isn't there yet.

Program open. Content support not there. The gap between those two things is where applications get lost before they start.

The opportunity: 6-8 dedicated pieces over 4-6 weeks - the problem, the mechanism, who it's for, what the process feels like - could fill spots before you need to think about this again.

03

You have at least 4 open series with no Part 2 or Part 3.

The apology series. The divorce series. Multiple posts that end with "Stay tuned for Part 2." The audiences who engaged with Part 1 are still there. The follow-up either came months later or didn't come at all. That's not a content problem - that's a capacity problem.

Series Part 1 posts regularly hit above-average engagement. The payoff for the audience who waited never arrived on time.

The opportunity: completing the top 3 open series generates 3 posts with a warm audience already waiting. No new topic needed. Just the close.
